Questions¶

Pulling the day of the week¶

.dt.dayofweek gives the day of the week in a numerical form. Online documentation says that 0 is Monday, and 6 is Sunday, so change that to the actual English for ease of readability. Do this with keys and values, creating a dictionary called dayOfWeek. The .map() method "translates" your numerical day using the dayOfWeek dictionary.

In [70]:
dayOfWeek={0:'Mon', 1:'Tue', 2:'Wed', 3:'Thu', 4:'Fri', 5:'Sat', 6:'Sun'}
speedingTicket['Day'] = speedingTicket['DateTime'].dt.dayofweek.map(dayOfWeek)

Answers to Questions 1 and 2¶

What day of the week are the MOST amount of speeding tickets written?

Tuesday.

What day of the week are the LEAST amount of speeding tickets written?

Sunday.

Intuitively, these answers make sense.

A significant portion of the population has Mondays off, so perhaps there are just less cops patrolling on Mondays. Alternatively; knowing that Monday is the start of the work week, potentially seeing the highest amount of traffic, officers don't have as many opportunities to catch people speeding. On Tuesday, most people, including the cops, are back to their normal work rhythm.

An overwhelming portion of the population has weekends off. Saturdays generally see significantly more traffic, as families go out and have fun. Sundays are typically slow (see "Lazy Sundays"), as there are nowhere near as many drivers on the road as on Saturdays.

Fixing the x axis ticks¶

Oh no! The hour ticks are unreadable! This is where the object-oriented interface of matplotlib comes into play, as I am only interested in making the x labels readable.

.xticks() calls the x ticks, and rotation rotates the labels by the degrees specified. Rotation also takes negative values to rotate counter-clockwise.

I also decided to rotate the y label for readability, and found that ylabel(rotation=-90) does not work because ylabel() requires an argument for the actual label, but I left it unchanged by using ylabel('count',rotation=0)

Answer to Question 3¶

At what hour are the MOST amount of speeding tickets written?

The 8am hour.

The 8am hour has the most amount of traffic tickets written. Most people work 9-to-5, frequently speeding to work when they are running late. Significantly fewer people start work at 10 or 11am. There is a lot less traffic after 9am, but cops are still looking to write tickets before their lunch break.

Answer to Question 5¶

Do men tend to drive faster than women?

Yes.

The base case in the regressions are female, where the intercepts show the average for each measure. All three of the coefficients for the males are positive. Given that they are already speeding, males will drive slightly faster at about 1.5mph over females. For any speed, males drive almost 3mph faster than females. Lastly, males choose a combination of routes whose average speed limit is about 1.5mph faster than the route a female would choose. Men drive faster than women, for reasons beyond the scope of this project.
